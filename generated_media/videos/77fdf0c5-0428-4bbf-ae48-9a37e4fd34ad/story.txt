The union of Roxana and Alexander the Great is emblematic of diplomatic and cultural integration. In 327 BC, this marriage took place amidst the breathtaking landscapes of Bactria, known today as Afghanistan. Roxana, renowned for her beauty and intellect, stood alongside Alexander, the Macedonian conqueror. He viewed her not only as a consort but as a symbolic bridge uniting the diverse cultures within his extensive empire. This union epitomized a strategic alliance designed to consolidate his empire, integrating Bactrian traditions into the fabric of his rule.
